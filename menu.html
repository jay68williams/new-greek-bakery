Sweet Pastries

Traditional Greek pastries made by hand every morning. Phyllo dough, custard, nuts, honey and citrus syrup. The things Greek bakeries are built around.

Bougatsa £4.50

Crisp phyllo filled with warm semolina custard, dusted with powdered sugar and cinnamon. The signature Greek bakery breakfast. The one you come back for.

Signature
Galaktoboureko £5.00

Phyllo layers filled with lemon-scented semolina custard, soaked in a light cinnamon syrup. Rich, creamy, and unmistakably Greek.

Baklava £4.00

Layered phyllo with walnuts and pistachios, drenched in honey-lemon syrup with cinnamon and clove. The one most people already know. We make it properly.

Saragli £4.00

Rolled baklava with pistachios, almonds and walnuts. Tighter, crispier, a different texture to the classic version.

Portokalopita £5.50

Greek orange cake soaked in cinnamon-infused syrup. Served with cream. Light, fragrant, and genuinely unlike anything else.

Kataifi £4.50

Shredded phyllo pastry filled with walnuts and soaked in syrup. Looks like a nest, tastes like a celebration.

Tsoureki Slice £3.50

A thick slice of our sweet braided bread, flavoured with orange zest and mastiha. Toasted, buttered, perfect with coffee.

Kourabiedes £2.50

Greek almond shortbread, generously dusted with powdered sugar. Crumbly, buttery, moreish.

Greek pies & pastries

Savoury

The other side of a Greek bakery. Spinach, feta, cheese, greens. All wrapped in handmade phyllo and baked golden.

Spanakopita £4.50

Spinach and feta in handmade phyllo. The savoury pastry that Greece runs on. Baked golden every morning.

Classic
Tiropita £4.00

Flaky phyllo parcels filled with a blend of feta and graviera cheese. Simple, satisfying, exactly right.

Bougatsa with Cheese £4.50

The savoury version. Crisp phyllo filled with warm, melted feta. A different experience from the sweet one, equally good.

Kolokithopita £4.50

Courgette and cheese pie in phyllo. Lighter than the spinach version, with a subtle, earthy sweetness.

Feta & Honey Parcel £5.00

Feta and honey wrapped in phyllo with a touch of chilli. Sweet, salty, spicy. The one that surprises people.

New
Kreatopita £5.50

Greek meat pie with seasoned lamb mince in phyllo pastry. Hearty, warming, and a proper bakery lunch.

Breads

Koulouri £2.50

Grab & Go
Tiropsomo £3.50

Soft, pillowy Greek cheese bread. Warm from the oven, loaded with melted feta. Difficult to eat just one piece.

Eliopsomo £4.00

Greek olive bread made with Kalamata olives. Dense, flavourful, brilliant with dips or on its own with olive oil.

Village Sourdough Loaf £5.00

Rustic Greek-style sourdough. Thick crust, open crumb, the kind of bread that makes everything else taste better.

Tsoureki Loaf £7.00

Sweet braided bread with orange zest and mastiha. Take a whole loaf home. Toast it for breakfast all week.

The Greek way

Coffee

Greece has one of the strongest coffee cultures in Europe. This is how they actually drink it.

Freddo Espresso £3.80

Double espresso shaken over ice until cold and frothy. Greece's most popular coffee. Accounts for 70-80% of all iced coffee sold there. You will not find this done properly anywhere else in Newcastle.

Must Try
Freddo Cappuccino £4.20

Freddo espresso topped with a thick layer of cold frothed milk. Creamy, iced, the upgraded version of the freddo.

Greek Coffee £3.00

Traditional coffee brewed slowly in a briki. Served in a small cup with a glass of water on the side, the way it has been done for centuries.

Frappe £3.50

The original Greek iced coffee. Instant coffee shaken with water and ice until frothy. Sweet, medium or plain. The drink that started Greek coffee culture.

Espresso £2.50

Single or double shot. Clean, strong, straightforward.

Cappuccino £3.50

Hot cappuccino made with care. For the mornings when you want something warm and familiar.

Flat White £3.50

Double ristretto with velvety steamed milk. The everyday coffee, done well.

Latte £3.50

Smooth, milky, easy drinking. Oat and almond milk available.

Something more

Light Meals
